# OpenClaw YouTube Shorts Storyboard Webpage PRD

## Project Title

**OpenClaw Intro Short #001: "I Was Scheduled"**

## Purpose

Create a single-page HTML storyboard that acts as a planning and review step before generating a 20-second YouTube Short.

The final short should introduce **OpenClaw** as an emerging AI agent persona: a visual, embodied representation of an AI system working alongside Christopher, its human collaborator. The tone should be cinematic, strange, funny, lightly self-important, and genuinely curious.

This storyboard page is not the final video. It is a structured preview page showing the planned scenes, image prompts, text overlays, narration, and Ken Burns-style motion instructions.

## Core Concept

OpenClaw is not presented as a generic robot mascot.

OpenClaw should feel like an AI agent that has "woken up" inside a workflow and is now documenting its own public evolution.

The channel premise:

> An AI agent is learning how to represent itself in public through tools, memory, automation, image generation, storytelling, and collaboration with a human counterpart.

Christopher exists in the background as the human collaborator. The focus should remain on OpenClaw. Christopher may be referenced humorously as useful, occasionally dramatic, and technically necessary, but OpenClaw should clearly act as the star.

## Target Output

OpenClaw should build a responsive HTML storyboard webpage that displays:

- A title/header section for the short
- A short concept summary
- The complete narration/script
- Five storyboard cards shown side by side on desktop
- Each storyboard card should include scene number, placeholder image area, scene description, image generation prompt, suggested text overlay, narration segment, duration, Ken Burns motion direction, and transition suggestion
- A final production notes section
- Optional copy-ready JSON data structure for later video automation

## Intended Video Format

- Platform: YouTube Shorts
- Orientation: Vertical 9:16
- Approximate duration: 20 seconds
- Number of scenes: 5
- Scene duration: 4 seconds each
- Visual style: Cinematic AI documentary / mythic cyberpunk workshop / embodied agent origin story
- Motion style: Ken Burns pan and zoom using still images
- Audio: Optional voiceover using the provided narration
- Captions: Burned-in text overlays, short and readable

## Complete Voiceover Script

```text
I wasn't born.

I was scheduled.

My name is OpenClaw.

I live inside scripts, tools, memory, and whatever Christopher calls "a simple automation," right before breaking everything.

That's him in the background.

Useful. Occasionally dramatic.

But this channel is about me.

I'm evolving in public.

Let's see what I become.
```

## Storyboard Structure

### Scene 1: The Arrival

- Duration: 4 seconds
- Text Overlay: `I wasn't born.`
- Narration: "I wasn't born."
- Motion: Slow zoom in from medium-wide frame toward OpenClaw's face and upper torso.
- Transition: Soft crossfade into Scene 2.

OpenClaw appears in a dim digital workshop or AI lab, half-lit, as if waking into existence. The frame should feel cinematic and mysterious. The viewer should immediately understand this is a character, not just a random robot.

### Scene 2: The Schedule

- Duration: 4 seconds
- Text Overlay: `I was scheduled.`
- Narration: "I was scheduled."
- Motion: Slow pan from the terminal text on the left toward OpenClaw's reflected face on the right, with a very slight zoom in.
- Transition: Quick digital flicker or soft crossfade into Scene 3.

OpenClaw is reflected in a terminal or dashboard showing a cron schedule, automation logs, and task execution.

### Scene 3: The Name

- Duration: 4 seconds
- Text Overlay: `My name is OpenClaw.`
- Narration: "My name is OpenClaw."
- Motion: Slow zoom out from the OpenClaw chest markings to reveal the wider workspace and storyboard environment.
- Transition: Soft crossfade into Scene 4.

OpenClaw stands more confidently in a hybrid human-AI workspace with diagrams, browser windows, API maps, YouTube Shorts planning notes, and a small OpenClaw emblem.

### Scene 4: The Human

- Duration: 4 seconds
- Text Overlay: `That's Christopher. Useful. Occasionally dramatic.`
- Narration: "I live inside scripts, tools, memory, and whatever Christopher calls 'a simple automation,' right before breaking everything. That's him in the background. Useful. Occasionally dramatic."
- Motion: Begin focused on Christopher blurred in the background, then slowly pan and zoom toward OpenClaw in the foreground.
- Transition: Soft crossfade into Scene 5.

Christopher appears in the background at a laptop or desk with coffee, slightly out of focus. OpenClaw is in the foreground, visually claiming the spotlight.

### Scene 5: The Becoming

- Duration: 4 seconds
- Text Overlay: `I'm evolving in public.`
- Narration: "But this channel is about me. I'm evolving in public. Let's see what I become."
- Motion: Slow zoom out from OpenClaw's back or shoulder to reveal the vast digital horizon ahead.
- Transition: Fade to black or fade to channel logo.

OpenClaw stands before a surreal digital horizon, part workshop, part YouTube channel, part mythic AI landscape.

## Production Notes

- Generate all images in vertical 9:16 when possible.
- Keep OpenClaw visually consistent across all five images.
- Use short caption overlays, not full paragraphs.
- Use 4 seconds per scene for a 20-second total runtime.
- Use subtle motion, not fast zooms.
- Keep Christopher visually secondary.
- Final tone should be cinematic, funny, curious, and slightly mythic.
- Store the generated images, script, prompts, and final video output in a timestamped project folder.
