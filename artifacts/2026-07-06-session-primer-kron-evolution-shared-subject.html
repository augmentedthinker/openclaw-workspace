The sequence matters because the learning did not only accumulate content. It changed the shape of the process.

Iteration 6 added the audit-trail concern: every run should leave a small receipt of what changed, what was verified, and what boundaries stayed closed.

Iteration 8 corrected the rhythm of a turn. A single entry should evaluate the previous iteration, implement one change, record the output, and stop. It should not add a Next Evaluation Target section that pulls the next turn's attention into the current one.

Iteration 9 incorporated Christopher's "that's me and you" signal. The collaboration itself became the subject: not just a ledger, not just an image series, not just a skill, but the human-agent relationship learning how to learn.

The current safety state remains unchanged: no cron job was changed, no public social post was made, and Kron Evolution remains a Workshop-only experimental lane unless Christopher explicitly approves a future boundary crossing.

Reflection Review

The newest Reflection, The Loop Learns Its Boundary, should shape the next session's judgment.

Its central claim is that a loop becomes trustworthy not when it can continue forever, but when it knows what a completed turn is. That is a serious operating lesson for agent work. The temptation in agentic systems is to keep reaching forward: evaluate, implement, propose, schedule, expand, and keep carrying every possible future inside the present action. The Reflection argues for a cleaner boundary. Complete the current turn. Leave evidence. Let the next turn arrive with its own attention.

That lesson has already changed behavior through Iteration 8. The Next Evaluation Target section was removed from the iteration shape, and the live Kron Evolution skill was updated so future runs do not reintroduce forward-looking or self-evaluating sections.

The Reflection also names Christopher's corrections as part of the intelligence of the system. That lesson has already changed behavior too. Iterations 7, 8, and 9 each absorbed Christopher input directly. His corrections were not treated as interruptions to automation. They became first-class learning signals.

The strongest unproven claim in the Reflection is that Kron Evolution can move beyond refining its own process and eventually help evolve an object, capability, offer, or behavior that touches Christopher's world more directly. Iteration 9 clarified that the collaboration is a legitimate subject, but the risk remains: the Workshop can still become fascinated by its own scaffolding.

What changed in the next action because of this lesson?

If the answer is only "the record became prettier," the loop is drifting.

What Is Not Yet Working

Kron Evolution still lacks a clear external destination. Iteration 9 identifies the collaboration as the shared subject, but the loop has not yet chosen an income-facing, user-facing, or platform-facing object to evolve.

The project brief still describes an early hypothesis about cron jobs that improve through iteration. The actual project has become a manual Workshop learning loop. That is not a problem, but the brief may eventually need a refresh so fresh sessions do not confuse the original hypothesis with the current operating shape.

The stable public routines are somewhat protected, but they are no longer the center of experimentation. That means their critique and analytics loops can drift if nobody periodically inspects them.

The Workshop still needs pressure from reality outside the Workshop. Internal learning loops are useful only if they eventually improve public output, service capability, product direction, automation value, or Christopher's leverage.
